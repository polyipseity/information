{@{The __gradient theorem__, also known as the __fundamental theorem of calculus for line integrals__}@}, says that {@{a [line integral](line%20integral.md) through a [gradient field](conservative%20vector%20field.md) can be evaluated by evaluating the original scalar field at the endpoints of the curve}@}. The theorem is {@{a generalization of the [second fundamental theorem of calculus](fundamental%20theorem%20of%20calculus.md)}@} to {@{any curve in a plane or space \(generally _n_-dimensional\) rather than just the real line}@}. <!--SR:!fsrs,2029-11-09T00:00:00.000Z,1225,1225.46601476,1,2,9,0,0,2026-07-03T00:00:00.000Z!fsrs,2029-10-21T00:00:00.000Z,1210,1210.28222352,1,2,9,0,0,2026-06-29T00:00:00.000Z!fsrs,2029-07-09T10:06:58.248Z,1130,1130.13417601,1,2,9,0,0,2026-06-05T10:06:58.248Z!fsrs,2029-08-17T16:25:37.021Z,1162,1161.75102421,1,2,9,0,0,2026-06-12T16:25:37.021Z-->

{@{The gradient theorem}@} implies that {@{line integrals through gradient fields are [path-independent](conservative%20vector%20field.md#path%20independence)}@}. ## proof

If {@{_φ_ is a [differentiable function](differentiable%20function.md) from some [open subset](open%20set.md) _U_ ⊆ __R__<sup>_n_</sup> to __R__}@} and {@{__r__ is a differentiable function from some closed [interval](interval%20(mathematics).md) \[_a_, _b_\] to _U_}@} \(Note that __r__ is {@{differentiable at the interval endpoints _a_ and _b_}@}. To do this, __r__ is {@{defined on an interval that is larger than and includes \[_a_, _b_\]}@}.\), then by {@{the [multivariate chain rule](chain%20rule.md#multivariable%20case)}@}, {@{the [composite function](function%20composition.md) _φ_ ∘ __r__}@} is {@{differentiable on \[_a_, _b_\]}@}: {@{$${\frac {\mathrm {d} }{\mathrm {d} t} }(\varphi \circ \mathbf {r} )(t)=\nabla \varphi (\mathbf {r} (t))\cdot \mathbf {r} '(t)$$}@} for {@{all _t_ in \[_a_, _b_\]}@}. Now suppose {@{the domain _U_ of _φ_ contains the differentiable curve _γ_ with endpoints __p__ and __q__}@}. \(This is {@{[oriented](orientation%20(vector%20space).md) in the direction from __p__ to __q__}@}\). ## examples

### example 1

Suppose _γ_ ⊂ __R__<sup>2</sup> is {@{the circular arc oriented counterclockwise from \(5, 0\) to \(−4, 3\)}@}. ### example 2

## converse of the gradient theorem

{@{Many of the critical theorems of vector calculus}@} {@{generalize elegantly to statements about the [integration of differential forms](differential%20form.md#integration) on [manifolds](differentiable%20manifold.md)}@}. In the language of {@{[differential forms](differential%20form.md) and [exterior derivatives](exterior%20derivative.md)}@}, the gradient theorem states that {@{$$\int _{\partial \gamma }\phi =\int _{\gamma }\mathrm {d} \phi$$}@} for {@{any [0-form](differential%20form.md), _ϕ_, defined on some differentiable curve _γ_ ⊂ __R__<sup>_n_</sup>}@} \(here {@{the integral of _ϕ_ over the boundary of the _γ_}@} is understood to be {@{the evaluation of _ϕ_ at the endpoints of _γ_}@}\). <!--SR:!fsrs,2029-09-29T00:00:00.000Z,1195,1195.07164214,1,2,9,0,0,2026-06-22T00:00:00.000Z!fsrs,2029-07-25T00:34:05.335Z,1145,1145.44606232,1,2,9,0,0,2026-06-06T00:34:05.335Z!fsrs,2029-10-20T00:00:00.000Z,1210,1210.28222352,1,2,9,0,0,2026-06-28T00:00:00.000Z!2027-11-12,592,310!fsrs,2029-10-30T00:00:00.000Z,1218,1217.87747351,1,2,9,0,0,2026-06-30T00:00:00.000Z!fsrs,2029-07-17T10:07:52.045Z,1138,1138.36935767,1,2,9,0,0,2026-06-05T10:07:52.045Z!fsrs,2029-07-14T10:05:09.989Z,1135,1135.03121845,1,2,9,0,0,2026-06-05T10:05:09.989Z-->

{@{The converse statement of the gradient theorem}@} also has {@{a powerful generalization in terms of differential forms on manifolds}@}. Thus, on {@{a contractible domain}@}, {@{every [closed](closed%20and%20exact%20differential%20forms.md) form is [exact](closed%20and%20exact%20differential%20forms.md)}@}.
