# ionization energy

__Ionization energy__ (__IE__) is {@{the minimum [energy](energy.md) needed to discharge an [electron](electron.md) from an isolated gaseous [atom](atom.md), [cation](ion.md), or [molecule](molecule.md)}@}. <!--SR:!2029-02-06,1582,290-->

## Bohr model

The ionization energy of [hydrogen](hydrogen.md) atom ($Z=1$) can be calculated from the [Bohr model](Bohr%20model.md#electron%20energy%20levels):
{@{$$E=-\frac{Z^2k_\mathrm{e}^2\mathrm{e}^4m_\mathrm{e} }{2\hbar^2n^2}\approx\frac{-13.6}{n^2}~\mathrm{eV}$$}@}
The ionization energy for hydrogen atom is simply {@{$I=E_\infty-E_1=13.6~\mathrm{eV}$}@}. <!--SR:!2026-05-24,652,230!2029-02-18,1667,310-->
