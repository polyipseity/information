# Young's modulus

__Young's modulus__ (or __Young modulus__) is {@{a mechanical property of solid materials that measures the tensile or compressive [stiffness](stiffness.md) when a [force](force.md) is applied uniaxially}@}. <!--SR:!2027-03-04,781,330-->

## definition

Young's modulus {@{$E$}@} quantifies {@{the relationship between tensile or compressive [stress](stress%20(mechanics).md) $\sigma$ and axial [strain](strain%20(mechanics).md) $\varepsilon$}@} in the {@{[linear elastic](linear%20elasticity.md) region of a material (within the elastic limit)}@}: {@{$$E = \frac \sigma \varepsilon$$}@} <!--SR:!2025-05-30,291,330!2025-09-22,338,290!2026-08-27,636,330!2026-08-24,581,310-->

The higher the Young's modulus, the {@{[stiffer](stiffness.md) it is}@}, and vice versa. The modulus has units {@{N/m<sup>2</sup>}@}. <!--SR:!2025-03-19,235,330!2026-09-01,636,330-->
