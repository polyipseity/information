# September equinox

The __September equinox__ or {{__southward equinox__}} is {{the [equinox](equinox.md) on the [Earth](Earth.md) when the [subsolar point](subsolar%20point.md) appears to head [southwards](south.md) to cross the [celestial equator](celestial%20equator.md) from the [Northern Hemisphere](Northern%20Hemisphere.md) to the [Southern Hemisphere](Southern%20Hemisphere.md)}}. It is also known as {{the fall or autumnal equinox in the Northern Hemisphere and as the spring or vernal equinox in the Southern Hemisphere}}. <!--SR:!2025-03-13,188,310!2024-09-16,50,270!2025-01-07,126,290-->

On {{the [Gregorian calendar](Gregorian%20calendar.md)}}, the September equinox can occur {{as early as September 21 and as late as September 24 in terms of [Universal Time](Universal%20Time.md)}}. <!--SR:!2024-10-05,70,310!2024-10-28,46,250-->
