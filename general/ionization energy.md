# ionization energy

__Ionization energy__ (__IE__) is {{the minimum [energy](energy.md) needed to discharge an [electron](electron.md) from an isolated gaseous [atom](atom.md), [cation](ion.md), or [molecule](molecule.md)}}. <!--SR:!2024-10-04,420,270-->

## Bohr model

The ionization energy of [hydrogen](hydrogen.md) atom ($Z=1$) can be calculated from the [Bohr model](Bohr%20model.md#electron%20energy%20levels):
{{$$E=-\frac{Z^2k_\mathrm{e}^2\mathrm{e}^4m_\mathrm{e} }{2\hbar^2n^2}\approx\frac{-13.6}{n^2}~\mathrm{eV}$$}}
The ionization energy for hydrogen atom is simply {{$I=E_\infty-E_1=13.6~\mathrm{eV}$}}. <!--SR:!2024-08-09,284,230!2024-07-24,414,290-->
