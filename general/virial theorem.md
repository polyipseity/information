# virial theorem

> __virial theorem__
>
> 1. {{For a stable system of $N$ discrete particles bound by a [conservative force](conservative%20force.md) $\vec{F}$,}}
> 2. {{$$\langle K_N \rangle = -\frac12 \sum_{i=1}^N \langle \vec{F}_i \cdot \vec{r}_i \rangle$$}}
>
> where
>
> - {{$K_N$ is the total [kinetic energy](kinetic%20energy.md) of the $N$ particles}}
> - {{$\vec{F}_i$ is the [force](force.md) on the $i$-th particle}}
> - {{$\vec{r}_i$ is the [position](position%20(geometry).md) of the $i$-th particle}} <!--SR:!2024-05-14,102,290!2024-03-12,57,250!2024-10-27,244,330!2024-09-14,193,310!2024-03-10,67,310-->

## examples

For [central forces](central%20force.md) {{in the form of $F \propto r^n$, the virial theorem simplifies to $\langle K_\mathrm{total} \rangle = \frac{n + 1}2 \langle U \rangle$}}. Particularly, for {{[gravity](gravitational%20potential.md) and [electrostatic force](Coulomb's%20law.md), for which $n = -2$, $\langle K_\mathrm{total} \rangle = -\frac12 \langle U \rangle$}}. <!--SR:!2024-06-03,104,257!2024-03-14,60,277-->
