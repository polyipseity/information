# ice I<sub>h</sub>

> {{![Structure of ice I<sub>h</sub>](../archives/Wikimedia%20Commons/Cryst%20struct%20ice.png)}}
>
> {{[crystal structure](crystal%20structure.md) of ice I<sub>h</sub>}} <!--SR:!2023-11-08,164,316!2023-11-28,181,316-->

__Ice I<sub>h</sub>__ is {{the hexagonal [crystal](crystal.md) form of [ice](ice.md)}}, which is the most common on [Earth](Earth.md). <!--SR:!2023-08-30,99,270-->

It is charaterized by {{the [hexagonal arrangement](hexagonal%20crystal%20family.md) of [oxygen](oxygen.md) atoms and [tetrahedral](tetrahedron.md) bond angles}}. <!--SR:!2023-09-02,101,270-->

## physical properties

Ice I<sub>h</sub> has {{lower [density](density.md) than [water](water.md) due to [hydrogen bonds](hydrogen%20bond.md) that make the [molecules](molecule.md) closer in the [liquid](liquid.md) phase}}. <!--SR:!2024-05-24,269,270-->
