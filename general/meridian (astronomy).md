# meridian

In [astronomy](astronomy.md), the __meridian__ is {@{the [great circle](great%20circle.md) passing through the [celestial poles](celestial%20pole.md), as well as the [zenith](zenith.md) and [nadir](nadir.md) of an observer's location}@}. Consequently, it contains {@{also the [north](true%20north.md) and [south](south.md) points on the [horizon](horizon.md)}@}, and it is [perpendicular](perpendicular.md) to {@{the [celestial equator](celestial%20equator.md) and horizon}@}.

> [!tip] tips
>
> - [a.m., p.m.](12-hour%20clock.md), and the meridian ::@:: a.m. is from [Latin](Latin.md) _ante meridiem_, meaning "before midday". p.m. is from [Latin](Latin.md) _post meridiem_, meaning "after midday".
