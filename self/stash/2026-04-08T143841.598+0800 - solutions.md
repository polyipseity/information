# secondary 3 mathematics - solutions@2026-04-08

## probability

1. **A bag contains 999 red counters and 1 blue counter. Two counters are drawn without replacement. What is the probability of drawing exactly one blue counter?**

   Either blue first, then red OR red first, then blue:
   - P(blue then red) = $\frac{1}{1000} \times \frac{999}{999} = \frac{1}{1000}$
   - P(red then blue) = $\frac{999}{1000} \times \frac{1}{999} = \frac{1}{1000}$
   - **Total: $\frac{1}{1000} + \frac{1}{1000} = \frac{1}{500}$**

2. **A fair die is rolled 1200 times. The face 6 appears 214 times. What is the experimental probability of getting a 6?**

   Experimental probability = $\frac{\text{times 6 appears}}{\text{total rolls}} = \frac{214}{1200}$
   - **Simplified: $\frac{107}{600}$ (or ≈ 0.178)**

3. **A number from 1 to 1000 is chosen at random. What is the probability that it is a multiple of 6 or 10?**

   Use inclusion-exclusion principle:
   - Multiples of 6: $\lfloor 1000 \div 6 \rfloor = 166$
   - Multiples of 10: $\lfloor 1000 \div 10 \rfloor = 100$
   - Multiples of both (LCM(6,10)=30): $\lfloor 1000 \div 30 \rfloor = 33$
   - Total: $166 + 100 - 33 = 233$
   - **Answer: $\frac{233}{1000}$**

4. **A bag contains 8 red, 9 blue, and 13 green balls. One ball is chosen, replaced, and then another is chosen. What is the probability that the two balls are the same colour?**

   Total balls = 30. With replacement, each draw is independent:
   - P(both red) = $\left(\frac{8}{30}\right)^2 = \frac{64}{900}$
   - P(both blue) = $\left(\frac{9}{30}\right)^2 = \frac{81}{900}$
   - P(both green) = $\left(\frac{13}{30}\right)^2 = \frac{169}{900}$
   - **Total: $\frac{64 + 81 + 169}{900} = \frac{314}{900} = \frac{157}{450}$ (or ≈ 0.349)**

5. **Two fair coins are tossed. This experiment is repeated 200 times. About how many times would you expect exactly one head?**

   Possible outcomes: HH, HT, TH, TT
   - P(exactly one head) = P(HT) + P(TH) = $\frac{1}{4} + \frac{1}{4} = \frac{1}{2}$
   - Expected frequency = $200 \times \frac{1}{2} = \textbf{100 times}$

## more about 3-D figures

1. **A cube of side 30 cm is cut into 27 equal smaller cubes. By what factor does the total surface area increase?**

   - $27 = 3^3$, so the cube is cut 3×3×3
   - Each small cube has side = $30 \div 3 = 10$ cm
   - Original surface area = $6 \times 30^2 = 5400$ cm²
   - New total surface area = $27 \times 6 \times 10^2 = 16200$ cm²
   - **Factor: $\frac{16200}{5400} = \mathbf{3}$ times**

2. **A cuboid has dimensions 40 cm by 50 cm by 60 cm. How many 10 cm cubes can fit exactly inside it?**

   Divide each dimension by 10:
   - Along length: $40 \div 10 = 4$
   - Along width: $50 \div 10 = 5$
   - Along height: $60 \div 10 = 6$
   - **Total: $4 \times 5 \times 6 = \mathbf{120}$ cubes**

3. **Two similar solids have linear scale factor 3:5. If the smaller solid has volume 216 cm³, what is the volume of the larger solid?**

   When linear scale factor is 3:5, volume scale factor is $\left(\frac{5}{3}\right)^3 = \frac{125}{27}$
   - Larger volume = $216 \times \frac{125}{27} = 8 \times 125 = \mathbf{1000}$ cm³

4. **Two cubes of side 12 cm are joined face to face. How much surface area is hidden after joining?**

   When two faces touch, they are no longer part of the surface:
   - Area of one face = $12^2 = 144$ cm²
   - **Total hidden: $2 \times 144 = \mathbf{288}$ cm²**

5. **A rectangular tank measures 1.2 m by 0.8 m by 0.5 m. How many litres of water can it hold?**

   Volume = $1.2 \times 0.8 \times 0.5 = 0.48$ m³
   - Convert to litres: $0.48 \times 1000 = \mathbf{480}$ litres

## mensuration (III)

1. **A circle has circumference 528 cm. Find its radius.**

   Using $C = 2\pi r$:
   - $r = \frac{C}{2\pi} = \frac{528}{2\pi} = \frac{264}{\pi}$
   - **$\mathbf{r \approx 84}$ cm** (using $\pi \approx 3.14$)

2. **A sector has radius 60 cm and angle 18°. Find the arc length.**

   Arc length = $\frac{\theta}{360°} \times 2\pi r = \frac{18}{360} \times 2\pi \times 60$
   - $= \frac{1}{20} \times 120\pi = 6\pi$ cm
   - **$\approx \mathbf{18.85}$ cm**

3. **A cone has radius 21 cm and slant height 29 cm. Find its curved surface area.**

   Curved surface area = $\pi rl = \pi \times 21 \times 29 = 609\pi$ cm²
   - **$\approx \mathbf{1913}$ cm²**

4. **A cylinder has radius 35 cm and height 1.2 m. Find its volume in litres.**

   Height = $1.2$ m $= 120$ cm
   - Volume = $\pi r^2 h = \pi \times 35^2 \times 120 = 147000\pi$ cm³
   - $\approx 461,814$ cm³ or $\approx \mathbf{461.8}$ **litres**

5. **A hemisphere has radius 50 cm. Find its total surface area.**

   Total surface area = curved surface + circular base = $2\pi r^2 + \pi r^2 = 3\pi r^2$
   - $= 3\pi \times 50^2 = 7500\pi$ cm²
   - **$\approx \mathbf{23,562}$ cm²**

## trigonometric relations

1. **In a right triangle, the hypotenuse is 250 cm and one acute angle is 37°. Find the length of the side opposite the angle.**

   Opposite = hypotenuse × sin(angle)
   - $= 250 \times \sin(37°) \approx 250 \times 0.602$
   - **$\approx \mathbf{150.5}$ cm**

2. **A ladder 9 m long makes an angle of 68° with the ground. How high up the wall does it reach?**

   Height = ladder × sin(angle) where angle is from ground
   - $= 9 \times \sin(68°) \approx 9 \times 0.927$
   - **$\approx \mathbf{8.34}$ m**

3. **In triangle ABC, AB=120 cm, AC=200 cm, and ∠A=120°. Find BC.**

   Using cosine rule: $BC^2 = AB^2 + AC^2 - 2 \cdot AB \cdot AC \cdot \cos(A)$
   - $BC^2 = 120^2 + 200^2 - 2(120)(200)\cos(120°)$
   - $BC^2 = 14400 + 40000 - 48000(-0.5)$
   - $BC^2 = 14400 + 40000 + 24000 = 78400$
   - **BC = $\mathbf{280}$ cm**

4. **In triangle PQR, PQ=18 cm, PR=30 cm, and QR=24 cm. Find the largest angle.**

   The largest angle is opposite the longest side. PR = 30 cm is longest, so angle Q is largest.
   - Using cosine rule: $PR^2 = PQ^2 + QR^2 - 2(PQ)(QR)\cos(Q)$
   - $900 = 324 + 576 - 2(18)(24)\cos(Q)$
   - $900 = 900 - 864\cos(Q)$
   - $\cos(Q) = 0$
   - **$\mathbf{\angle Q = 90°}$ (this is a right triangle!)**

5. **In triangle XYZ, ∠X=35°, ∠Y=65°, and XZ=180 cm. Find YZ.**

   First: $\angle Z = 180° - 35° - 65° = 80°$

   Using sine rule: $\frac{YZ}{\sin X} = \frac{XZ}{\sin Y}$
   - $YZ = \frac{XZ \times \sin X}{\sin Y} = \frac{180 \times \sin(35°)}{\sin(65°)}$
   - $YZ = \frac{180 \times 0.574}{0.906} \approx \frac{103.3}{0.906}$
   - **$\approx \mathbf{114}$ cm**
