# secondary 3 mathematics@2026-04-08

## probability

1. A bag contains $999$ red counters and $1$ blue counter. Two counters are drawn without replacement. What is the probability of drawing exactly one blue counter?
2. A fair die is rolled $1200$ times. The face $6$ appears $214$ times. What is the experimental probability of getting a $6$?
3. A number from $1$ to $1000$ is chosen at random. What is the probability that it is a multiple of $6$ or $10$?
4. A bag contains $8$ red, $9$ blue, and $13$ green balls. One ball is chosen, replaced, and then another is chosen. What is the probability that the two balls are the same colour?
5. Two fair coins are tossed. This experiment is repeated $200$ times. About how many times would you expect exactly one head?

## more about 3-D figures

1. A cube of side $30$ cm is cut into $27$ equal smaller cubes. By what factor does the total surface area increase?
2. A cuboid has dimensions $40$ cm by $50$ cm by $60$ cm. How many $10$ cm cubes can fit exactly inside it?
3. Two similar solids have linear scale factor $3:5$. If the smaller solid has volume $216$ cm<sup>3</sup>, what is the volume of the larger solid?
4. Two cubes of side $12$ cm are joined face to face. How much surface area is hidden after joining?
5. A rectangular tank measures $1.2$ m by $0.8$ m by $0.5$ m. How many litres of water can it hold?

## mensuration (III)

1. A circle has circumference $528$ cm. Find its radius.
2. A sector has radius $60$ cm and angle $18^\circ$. Find the arc length.
3. A cone has radius $21$ cm and slant height $29$ cm. Find its curved surface area.
4. A cylinder has radius $35$ cm and height $1.2$ m. Find its volume in litres.
5. A hemisphere has radius $50$ cm. Find its total surface area.

## trigonometric relations

1. In a right triangle, the hypotenuse is $250$ cm and one acute angle is $37^\circ$. Find the length of the side opposite the angle.
2. A ladder $9$ m long makes an angle of $68^\circ$ with the ground. How high up the wall does it reach?
3. In triangle $ABC$, $AB=120$ cm, $AC=200$ cm, and $\angle A=120^\circ$. Find $BC$.
4. In triangle $PQR$, $PQ=18$ cm, $PR=30$ cm, and $QR=24$ cm. Find the largest angle.
5. In triangle $XYZ$, $\angle X=35^\circ$, $\angle Y=65^\circ$, and $XZ=180$ cm. Find $YZ$.
