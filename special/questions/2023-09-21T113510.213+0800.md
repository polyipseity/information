# 2023-09-21T113510.213+0800

Let $f(x)=\frac1{x+8}$. $\frac{f(10+h)-f(10)}h$ can be simplified to $-\frac 1 {ah+b}$. Find $a$ and $b$.

## strategy

- inspecting "Let $f(x)=\frac1{x+8}$. $\frac{f(10+h)-f(10)}h$ can be simplified to $-\frac 1 {ah+b}$." :@: Aggressively simplify the expression to become 1 fraction only. <!--SR:!2025-04-19,198,290-->

## solution

$$\begin{aligned}
\frac{f(10+h)-f(10)}h&=\frac{\frac1{18+h}-\frac1{18} }h\\
&=\frac{18-18-h}{18(18+h)h}\\
&=-\frac 1 {18h+324}\\
(a,b)&=(18,324)
\end{aligned}$$
