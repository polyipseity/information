# 2023-12-05T183457.620+0800

Evaluate $$\lim_{x\to-1^+}\frac{\sin((x+1)(x+2))}{\lvert{x^2-1}\rvert}$$.

## strategy

- inspecting "$$\lim_{x\to-1^+}\frac{\sin((x+1)(x+2))}{\lvert{x^2-1}\rvert}$$" :: Simplify $\lvert x^2 - 1 \rvert$ to $1 - x^2$ considering $x$ approaches $-1$ from above. Expand $1 - x^2$ to $(1 - x)(x + 1)$. <!--SR:!2025-09-20,317,290-->
- solving $$\lim_{x\to-1^+}\frac{\sin((x+1)(x+2))}{(1 - x)(x + 1)}$$ :: Force it by multiplying both numerator and denominator by $x + 2$. Then $\lim_{x \to -1^+} \frac {\sin((x + 1)(x + 2))} {(x + 1)(x + 2)} = 1$. <!--SR:!2025-08-08,304,330-->

## solution

$$\begin{aligned}
\lim_{x\to-1^+}\frac{\sin((x+1)(x+2))}{\lvert{x^2-1}\rvert}&=\lim_{x\to-1^+}\frac{\sin((x+1)(x+2))}{1-x^2}\\
&=\lim_{x\to-1^+}\frac{\sin((x+1)(x+2))}{(1+x)(1-x)}\\
&=\lim_{x\to-1^+}\frac{(x+2)\sin((x+1)(x+2))}{(x+1)(x+2)(1-x)}\\
&=\lim_{x\to-1^+}\frac{x+2}{1-x}\\
&=\frac{(-1)+2}{1-(-1)}\\
&=\frac12
\end{aligned}$$
