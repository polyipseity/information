# 2023-10-03T125523.090+0800

Find an equation of the tangent to the curve $x^6+4xy+y^2=6$ at the point $(1,1)$.

## strategy

- inspecting "Find an equation of the tangent to the curve $x^6+4xy+y^2=6$ at the point $(1,1)$." :: Implicit differentiation with respect to $x$. <!--SR:!2024-07-06,4,270-->

## solution

$$\begin{aligned}
x^6+4xy+y^2&=6\\
6x^5+4(y+xy')+2y'y&=0\\
6x^5+4y+4xy'+2y'y&=0\\
(4x+2y)y'&=-6x^5-4y\\
y'&=-\frac{3x^5+2y}{2x+y}\\
y'(1,1)&=-\frac{3+2}{2+1}\\
&=-\frac53\\
\frac{y-1}{x-1}&=-\frac53\\
3y-3&=-5x+5\\
y&=\frac{-5x+8}3
\end{aligned}$$
