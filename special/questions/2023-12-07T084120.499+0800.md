Given a [twice-differentiable](differentiable%20function.md) $f$, prove that if $f(-x)=-f(x)$, then $f''(-x)=-f''(x)$.

> [!success]- solution
>
> $$\begin{aligned}
f'(x)&=\lim_{h\to0}\frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}h\\
f'(-x)&=\lim_{h\to0}\frac{f(-x+h)-f(-x)}h\\
&=\lim_{h\to0}\frac{-f(x-h)+f(x)}h\\
&=\lim_{h\to0}\frac{-f(x+h)+f(x)}{-h}\\
&=\lim_{h\to0}\frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}h\\
&=f'(x)\\
f''(x)&=\lim_{h\to0}\frac{f'(x+h)-f'(x)}h\\
f''(-x)&=\lim_{h\to0}\frac{f'(-x+h)-f'(-x)}h\\
&=\lim_{h\to0}\frac{f'(x-h)-f'(x)}h\\
&=\lim_{h\to0}\frac{f'(x+h)-f'(x)}{-h}\\
&=-f''(x)
\end{aligned}$$
