Introduction

In this lab, you will try to solve a problem related to Perfect Numbers.

A perfect number is a positive integer that is equal to the sum of its positive divisors, excluding the number itself. Here are some examples,

  • 6 is a perfect number because 6 = 1 + 2 + 3.
  • 28 is a perfect number because 28 = 1 + 2 + 4 + 7 + 14.
  • 32 is not a perfect number because 32 != 1 + 2 + 4 + 8 + 16.
In this lab, you will write a program to find all the perfect numbers between two numbers (both ends inclusive).

Here we have some requirements for the lab:

  • Initially, our score is 0. If we find a perfect number, we add that number to the score. Otherwise, we subtract that number from the score.
  • You cannot include and use any other libraries and functions.

Checking Before Submission

Please compare your outputs with the desired outputs:

  • In VSCode, please open the terminal by one of the following two ways:
    • Press the key Ctrl+Shift+` in Windows/Linux, or Control+Shift+Backquote in Mac
    • From the menubar, select Terminal > New Terminal
    And then run this code to compile your code: 
    g++ -std=c++11 lab2.cpp -o lab2
  • You need to redirect the input of executable program to the txt test case files we provided, and redirect the output to different txt files. Here are the commands:
    • In MacOS or Linux: 
      ./lab2 < testcase/input1.txt > myOutput1.txt

      Get-Content testcase/input1.txt | ./lab2 > myOutput1.txt
