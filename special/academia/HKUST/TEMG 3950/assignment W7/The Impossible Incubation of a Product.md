# The Impossible Incubation of a Product

_Write an individual report (max 500 words), describing your own contributions to the project and reflection on the project._

_You can tell us your experiences, ideas, and observations related to the course. Have you developed knowledge and useful skills in this course?  What are the great challenges in doing this course?  You can also discuss how you developed product ideas, discussed with your teammates, solved a problem, and reached an agreement with your teammate on the choice of the final product idea. What are your recommendations for possible improvements?_

The project, to say the least, required immense effort to complete.

To design a product, one must consider its desirability, feasibility, and viability. It seems easy on paper, but actually achieving and balancing all three is a lot harder in practice. Identifying a problem to solve and considering the feasibility of possible solutions to said problem is already difficult enough. The real problems are usually ill-defined, making the possible solutions hard to evaluate.

And when you finally think of a great idea that is desirable and feasible, it fails on the viability part. The world is a very large place indeed, so large that almost any great idea is already solved and marketed by someone else. The marketplace of ideas is too full, and the only way to find the hidden gem is to spend even more time and effort. Unfortunately, the course only had 1.5 months for the project, which is nowhere near enough to do so. Instead, we had to compromise on the three factors of the product.

This is why I had spent so much time thinking of an idea myself, and yet I could not find any great ideas. I have to lower my standards. In the end, after much discussion and many meetings, our team adopted a translation app idea that is not mine, but even then we needed to rethink a new idea after the first review. I was not fully satisfied with both ideas, but I have eventually come to realize this is normal. In short: There are no perfect products, but only a product that is less imperfect than others.

I have come to appreciate the difficulty in designing a new product, realizing that I still need immense practice in order to make a workable product. In particular, I need to identify issues with my own ideas, which is a lot harder than it looks.

After we had confirmed our idea to make a smartwatch for dementia patients, we needed to make the idea concrete. And there are a lot of things to elaborate on: the design, business model, market research, etc. All of which required me to do extensive research on the internet. This is especially true when you have neither the technical specialty nor the business experience to refer to in your head. I must say, I have learnt quite a bit about making a smartwatch. While we have had many meetings (at 9 am!), it was barely enough to make the final product satisfactory.

Our team collaboration was great. We discussed a lot about the project on WhatsApp with little friction. I still feel like I should have participated more so that our final product is even better. Another improvement would be to actually test our ideas in the real world, but alas, we did not have the motivation to do so.

To conclude, was the project fun? I could not say that, but it gave me insight into the impossibility of incubating a product nowadays.

_497 words._
