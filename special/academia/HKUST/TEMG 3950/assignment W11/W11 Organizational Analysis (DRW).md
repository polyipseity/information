# Organizational Analysis (DRW)

This is an individual assignment. Past assignment example is provided here to level the playing field.  You are NOT allowed to approach other students for theirs past assignment, and penalty will be imposed if you do so - to both students.  <span style="color: #e03e2d;">Similarity % reported by TurnItIn will be subtracted from the final score.</span>

DRW Technologies is facing reduction in profitability from both customers segments of US defense sector and commercial airlines manufacturers.  A new <span style="color: #e03e2d;">position of corporate VP of Procurement was created to reduce spending and do so quickly. Ed Claiborne was hired for the job, and he rolled out a new policy to meet his goal of cost reduction</span>.  Nothing happened.

Please conduct situation analysis of the reactions to Claiborne's new procurement policies:

1. <u>Root Cause Analysis in Cause Mapping notation </u>(<u>2-3 slides</u>): key business issue(s) on the LHS, and root causes on the RHS. Incorporate dimensions from McKinsey's 7S framework so that your exploration of underlying causes is comprehensive. You should have >5Why's on each of the 7 dimensions, >7 "evidence boxes“ using facts from the case or your own imagination and >5 “solution boxes” to support steps in the RCA.  Attached is an imperfect example from the previous class to get you started but it many things may be inaccurate.  You can place 7 dimensions on no more than 3 slides. Somewhere on the slide, give a report card of your assessment of DRW's organizational capabilities by 7S dimensions: Red dot for "broken", Yellow dot for "slightly-broken" or Green dot for "OK".
2. <u>A table of solutions </u>(<u>1 slide</u>): describe your creative best 5 solutions to turn the situation around. Your "solutions" table should be based on information from the case, and you can supplement information using logical deduction and creativity with column headers of "No.", "Description", "Who", "Weeks" and "Budget". (e.g., "No." is 1 to <span style="color: #e03e2d;">5+;</span> "Description" is a short solution description; "Who" is the title of person who would be responsible for this solution strategy; and “Weeks" is your estimation of how many weeks it will take to develop the solution to pilot test complete stage, and "Budget" is your estimation of costs in USD.)
3. <u>An email in 5-Step Persuasive Selling format </u>(<u>1 slide</u>): your name is Bruce Lee, an external management consultant hired by the CEO to turn around the situation. Your email should be 200-250 words in >= 8 pt. font size.

Please submit a .pdf or .pptx file in landscape orientation.

## Q1

- <span style="color: white; background-color: red;">Symptom</span>: Failure of implementing new procurement policy
  - Plant managers not reporting contracts over $250k in advance of 2 weeks
    - (7S: Strategy) Contracts are time-sensitive
      - <span style="color: black; background-color: lightgreen;">Solution</span>: Shorten policy notification advance time to 1 week or even less
      - <span style="color: black; background-color: pink;">Evidence</span>: The plants are busy and many have added extra shifts
      - There are external rush orders
        - <span style="color: black; background-color: pink;">Evidence</span>: Plant executives felt that temporary external circumstances caused the target shortfalls
        - AND
          - External rush orders are by high-priority customers
          - Maintain relationships with high-priority customers
            - To retain loyal customers
    - (7S: Staff) Wrong person is hired for the position in the company
      - AND
        - Ed Claiborne is not the most suitable for the position in the company
          - <span style="color: black; background-color: pink;">Evidence</span>: Ed Claiborne ignored Lopez Debby's advice even though Debby has 14 years of experience
          - AND
            - The company chain of command is lax
            - Ed Claiborne had been working for a firm with a strict chain of command
        - Dagmar Hilgard did not consider hiring Ed Claiborne thoroughly
          - <span style="color: black; background-color: lightgreen;">Solution</span>: The candidate being appointed a high-level position also requires approval from the board of directors
          - <span style="color: black; background-color: pink;">Evidence</span>: Dagmar Hilgard hired Ed Claiborne despite the chief financial officer, Charles Suh, expressed reservations
          - Dagmar Hilgard has a primarily finance background instead of management background
    - (7S: Skills) Ed Claiborne has not adapted to the company management culture
      - AND
        - Ed Claiborne requires assistance to adapt to the company culture
          - AND
            - The company culture is decentralization
            - Ed Claiborne had been working for a hierarchical firm
        - Insufficient assistance for Ed Claiborne
          - <span style="color: black; background-color: lightgreen;">Solution</span>: Onboarding for new high-level employees
          - <span style="color: black; background-color: pink;">Evidence</span>: Ed Claiborne was already assigned work on his first day
          - Dagmar Hilgard trusts Ed Claiborne too much
            - <span style="color: black; background-color: pink;">Evidence</span>: Dagmar Hilgard did not need to know the details of policy implementation
    - (7S: Structure) Corporate Vice President of Procurement does not directly command the plant procurement departments
      - <span style="color: black; background-color: lightgreen;">Solution</span>: Restructure the company to make procurement more centralized
      - <span style="color: black; background-color: pink;">Evidence</span>: Plant procurement did not comply with the policy
      - It takes effort to reorganize the organization across plants
        - The organization is large
          - <span style="color: black; background-color: pink;">Evidence</span>: The company has 21 manufacturing plants in the United States
        - Resistance from plant managers
          - Plants have been having high autonomy for a long time
    - (7S: System) It is inconvenient to report contracts
      - No technology to integrate information across plants
        - <span style="color: black; background-color: lightgreen;">Solution</span>: Invest in information technology to manage plants
        - <span style="color: black; background-color: pink;">Evidence</span>: Plant managers informally share information on vendors with each other
        - The company has no technology department to improve internal efficiency
          - <span style="color: black; background-color: lightgreen;">Solution</span>: Setup a technology department
        - Lack of desire within the company
          - Plants are highly autonomous
          - Cooperation across plants are not actively encouraged
            - <span style="color: black; background-color: lightgreen;">Solution</span>: Setup incentives to encourage cooperation across plants
    - (7S: Style) Plant procurement managers do not respect the policy
      - AND
        - Plant procurement managers do not respect Ed Claiborne
          - Ed Claiborne is newly employed by the company
          - Ed Claiborne did not visit the plants
            - <span style="color: black; background-color: lightgreen;">Solution</span>: Ed Claiborne should visit some of the plants for suggestions on centralizing procurement
        - There is little penalty not following the policy
          - <span style="color: black; background-color: lightgreen;">Solution</span>: Impose small penalties for failing to follow policies
          - Plant management is highly autonomous
            - <span style="color: black; background-color: pink;">Evidence</span>: Several plants are allowed to miss their targets in the previous 3 years
    - (7S: Shared values) Ed Claiborne cannot effectively command others in the company
      - Others in the company do not like Ed Claiborne
        - Ed Claiborne is incompatible with the established company values
          - <span style="color: black; background-color: lightgreen;">Solution</span>: Company workshops to help realign values
          - AND
            - Ed Claiborne values control
              - <span style="color: black; background-color: pink;">Evidence</span>: Ed Claiborne had been working for a firm with a strict chain of command
            - The established company values are autonomy and decentralization
              - <span style="color: black; background-color: pink;">Evidence</span>: Plants have their own departments

## Q3

Dear Ms. Hilgard,

As you know, some plants missed their targets for the previous 3 years. Thus, the new Corporate Vice President of Procurement, Mr. Claiborne, introduced a new policy of notifying him of purchases over $250k in advance of 2 weeks. However, no one notified him of any purchases for the several weeks this policy is implemented. We found that bad policy implementation, lack of new employee onboarding, and insufficient information technology to be the root causes.

Our solution is improving policy implementation, better onboarding, and better information technology.

To help fix the new policy quickly, Mr. Claiborne can shorten the advance duration to 1 week or less. In the long term, he should visit the plants and ask the plant managers for advice on his policy. Meanwhile, you and the human resources department should help Mr. Claiborne and any new high-level employees with company onboarding. Finally, you and the board of directors should consider setting a technology department to invest in information technology.

Our solution above can help with compliance with the new policy and refine it. It can help new high-level employees avoid making mistakes. And the technology department can help with the company's digital transformation and improve operational efficiency. Digital transformation is the next big thing, and other companies are investing in technology too, so we should not fall behind and invest in technology.

I would like go over the details with you as soon as possible. Could we schedule a meeting? Thanks.

Sincerely,<br/>
Bruce Lee
