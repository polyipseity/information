__<u>Case summary:</u>__

In 1980, when Schultz met the original ___Starbucks Coffee___, ___Tea and Spice Ltd___. founded by 2 teachers and 1 writer, he knew it was something special. When Schultz visited Milan in 1983, his vision of what America was missing became clear - ___real coffee___! From 1985 to 2000, Schultz grew Starbucks domestically as well as in a few international markets to become a multi-billion company. In 2000, Schultz passed the baton to CFO, Orin Smith, and stepped down from CEO to chairman. In 2005, Jim Donald was hired from the retail industry as CEO. Donald executed aggressive growth strategies for further global expansion and product diversification, but distracted Starbucks from making its stores an inviting place with innovative products. Operating income tanked from US$1 billion in 2007 to US$504 million in 2008. In January 2008, Schultz took charge again as the CEO.

## Q3

Dear Mr. Schultz,

As you know, our company Starbucks has seen a decrease of operating income from US$1B in 2007 to US$504M in 2008. This is a worrying trend, so you have asked us to identify how to fix it. After much discussion within our committee, we have finally concluded.

Our solution is the 7 points of transformation, focusing on repairing our Starbucks branding, with a side focus of sustainable growth in the future.

The solution composes of 7 actions that the company should take. Each action comes with a clear description, persons responsible, duration, budget, and is labeled on the root cause analysis map. For example, the first action is to "be the undisputed coffee authority", which is to repair our branding. The Strategy Officer will shutdown shops that are affecting our branding, and the Marketing Officer will be responsible for the promotion. We estimated the pilot run will take 12 months and US$200M. We have also explained the benefits in the description.

By adopting our 7 points of transformation, we can prevent our operating income from decreasing further in a year. If executed well, we may be able to recover most of our operating income. Furthermore, it also prepares us the necessary conditions for future sustainable growth.

If you are interested, I would like to talk to you about our 7 points of transformation as soon as possible. Could you please reply with the closest free time that you have? Thank you.

Sincerely,<br/>
Bruce Lee
