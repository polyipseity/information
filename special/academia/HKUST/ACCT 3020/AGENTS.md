# ACCT 3020 agent instructions

__Course accounting standard: IFRS (International Financial Reporting Standards).__ This course teaches accounting under IFRS (IAS 1, IAS 32, IAS 33, etc.), not US GAAP. When teaching dividend measurement, equity account treatment, reserves, or financial instrument presentation, always give IFRS as the primary method and compare with US GAAP (ASC) where relevant. Key differences are highlighted in topical notes.

- Keep worked journal-entry examples in the nearest topical note instead of rebuilding a course-wide `journal entries.md` collector.
- When adding or editing an accounting entry, update the surrounding concept prose, the worked example blockquote, and the local flashcards in the same section.
- For ACCT 3020, the durable homes are currently `current liability.md` for short-term notes, AP-to-note conversion, income tax payable, and compensated absence; `provisions and contingencies.md` for warranty, premiums, ARO/decommissioning, onerous contracts, and restructuring; `non-current liabilities.md` for bonds, long-term notes, debt extinguishment, and debt modification; and `equity.md` for share issuance, preference-share issuance, dividends, and preference-dividend allocation patterns.
- In these topic notes, QA cards remain the default, but worked journal-entry examples may use cloze in quoted scenarios, tables, calculations, and short explanations.
- If you move or add a worked example, also update `index.md` so lecture routes point to the topical note rather than to a collector file.
