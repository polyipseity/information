# ACCT 3020 agent instructions

__Course accounting standard: IFRS (International Financial Reporting Standards).__ This course teaches accounting under IFRS (IAS 1, IAS 32, IAS 33, etc.), not US GAAP. When teaching dividend measurement, equity account treatment, reserves, or financial instrument presentation, always give IFRS as the primary method and compare with US GAAP (ASC) where relevant. Key differences are highlighted in topical notes.

- Do not use chapter numbers in prose, flashcards, routes, or local agent guidance. The `.tmp` source materials are not a durable navigation layer, so all durable references must use topic names and in-repo section links.
- Keep worked journal-entry examples in the nearest topical note instead of rebuilding a course-wide `journal entries.md` collector.
- When adding or editing an accounting entry, update the surrounding concept prose, the worked example blockquote, and the local flashcards in the same section.
- For ACCT 3020, the durable homes are currently `current liability.md` for short-term notes, AP-to-note conversion, income tax payable, and compensated absence; `provisions and contingencies.md` for warranty, premiums, ARO/decommissioning, onerous contracts, and restructuring; `non-current liabilities.md` for bonds, long-term notes, debt extinguishment, and debt modification; `equity.md` for share issuance, preference-share issuance, dividends, and preference-dividend allocation patterns; `dilutive securities and earnings per share.md` for convertibles, warrants, share-based compensation, and EPS; `investments.md` for debt investments, equity investments, impairment, derivatives, hedging, and swaps; `accounting for income taxes.md` for deferred tax logic; and `ESG reporting.md` for ESG framework and Hong Kong disclosure requirements.
- In these topic notes, QA cards remain the default, but worked journal-entry examples may use cloze in quoted scenarios, tables, calculations, and short explanations.
- In these topic notes, each section's worked journal-entry examples must stay above that section's flashcard block. After `Flashcards for this section are as follows:` begins, keep only flashcards until the next header.
- If you move or add a worked example, also update `index.md` so lecture routes point to the topical note rather than to a collector file.
- If a tutorial, exam, or office-hours question exposes a tricky case, exam trap, or "what if" variant, add it to the canonical topical note under a descriptive hierarchical subheading rather than creating a detached collector or leaving it only in `questions.md`.
- For accounting edge cases, keep the base rule, the exception, and the worked entry or calculation together in the same topical section or sub-section so the reader can see when the normal rule breaks.
- When a note gains enough edge cases to feel crowded, improve hierarchy with topic-based subheadings and route `index.md` directly to the most specific subsection.
