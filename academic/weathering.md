# weathering

__Weathering__ is {{the deterioration of [material](material.md) through contact with external agents _[in situ](in%20situ.md)_}}. It is distinct from {{[erosion](erosion.md) which involves movement}}.

Weathering can be divided into {{_[physical weathering](#physical%20weathering)_ and _[chemical weathering](#chemical%20weathering)_}}.

## physical weathering

### frost weathering

_Frost weathering_ or _frost action_ is {{weathering caused by formation of [ice](ice.md) in outcrops}}. In {{_forst wedging_}}, {{the expansion of pore [water](water.md) with it freezes fractures the surrounding rock}}.

### thermal stress

_Thermal stress weathering_ {{results from expansion and contraction of [rocks](rock%20(geology).md) due to [temperature](temperature.md) changes}}. It is important in {{[deserts](desert.md) as there is a large [diurnal](diurnal.md) temperature range}}.

## chemical weathering

### dissolution

_Dissolution_ is {{weathering in which a mineral [dissolves](dissolution.md) without producing any [solids](solids.md)}}.

Carbonate dissolution is {{dissolution in which atmospheric [carbon dioxide](carbon%20dioxide.md) enhances weathering}}. It affects rocks {{containing [calcium carbonate](calcium%20carbonate.md) like [limestone](limestone.md), [chalk](chalk.md), and [marble](marble.md)}}.
