# carbon dioxide

## tests

### calcium carbonate
> - ==CO<sub>2</sub>(g) + Ca(OH)<sub>2</sub>(s)== → ==CaCO<sub>3</sub>(s) + H<sub>2</sub>O(l)==
> - observation: ==White precipitates form.==
> - when: ==excess CO<sub>2</sub>(g) is passed into the [limewater](calcium%20hydroxide.md)==
>     - ==CaCO<sub>3</sub>(s) + CO<sub>2</sub>(g) + H<sub>2</sub>O(l)== → ==Ca(HCO<sub>3</sub>)<sub>2</sub>(aq)==
>     - observation: ==The white precipitates dissolve to give a clear solution again.==

### hydrogencarbonate indicator
> Add hydrogencarbonate indicator to the gas. If the gas is ==CO<sub>2</sub>==, the indicator could turn from ==red== to ==yellow==.
